Emeralds Value Assessment Process


The GEMERA Project built a model for setting the price in GEMERAS of the emeralds stored in a security vault of a safe deposit box company in Hong Kong and whose characteristics, pictures and video are hosted in GEMERA’s marketplace.

The price setting takes into consideration the tone, clarity, enhancement and carat weight of each one of the emeralds, as specified in GEMERA’s white paper (item 5).

Taking into account the previous parameters, the following are the steps to determine the price of the emeralds:


1. Tone Range


The first analysis considers the tone of the emeralds to grant them a quality grade, divided in Intense (the highest prices), Medium and Light (the lowest prices).


2. Enhancement Classification


Afterwards, the emeralds are classified according to the enhancement level, following the Quantification and Identification of Enhancement proposed by the Laboratory Manual Harmonisation Committee (LMHC) and the World Jewelry Confederation (CIBJO), which can be visited in GEMERA’s white paper (Quality Parameters, item 5.1.). The result following classification:


Enhancement Classification


E1 None, Insignificant
E2 Minor (F1)
E3 Moderate (F2)
E4 Significant (F3)

3. Clarity Classification


Then the emeralds undergo an analysis about how visible their inclusions are, and the result is classified in the following table (for more information about the concepts of ‘clarity’ and ‘inclusions’, please visit GEMERA’s White Paper, item 5):


Clarity Classification


CL 1 Eye Clean
CL 2 Slightly Included
CL 3 Moderately Included
CL 4 Heavily Included and Severely Included

5. Price Adjustment

The emeralds with cavities have a reduction on its price according to the enhancement level, as specified in the Emerald Quantification and Identification of Filled Cavities (GEMERA’s White Paper item 5.1.). This adjustment in the price is specified as follows:


Enhancement level on emeralds with cavities Price Reduction Percentage
C1 - Minor
 - 50%
C2 - Moderate
 - 60%
C3 - Significant
 - 75%


The price of the emeralds with a cabochon form will have a reduction of 10%. For more information about emeralds’ cut, visit GEMERA’s White Paper (item 5.1.).

Note: The previous model is the result of a market analysis of Colombian emeralds, with the support of a team of specialists in fine Colombian emeralds and following the parameters for tone and clarity of the GIA. The model might change through the time, whether significant changes in the market estimation on emeralds happen.
